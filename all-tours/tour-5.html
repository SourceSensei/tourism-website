Palaces of the Empire

National Palace of Ajuda. It was built at the beginning of the 19th century on the site of the royal palace, destroyed by fire in 1795. The royal family spared no expense! Silk wallpaper, Sèvres porcelain and crystal candelabra Pena Palace. On the peaks of the Serra di Sintra, stands the magnificent Pena Palace, an eclectic mix of architectural styles built in the 19th century. This is a magical place! Queluz Palace. In 1747, Pedro, the youngest son of João V, commissioned Mateus Vicenti to convert the hunting lodge into a rococo summer palace. Queluz is a typical Rococo palace with extensive French gardens, dazzlingly sparkling with newly restored chandeliers reflected in the numerous mirrors of the walls. These palaces are eloquent proof that for centuries Portugal was a huge Empire, and not the backyards of Europe! Shall we make sure of this together?
