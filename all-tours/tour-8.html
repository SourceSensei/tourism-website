Journey through the Middle Ages

We are leaving for the Middle Ages of Portugal. In the city of Tomar we will try to understand what unites the history of Portugal with the mysterious history of the Templars. In Batalha we will visit the Dominican abbey of Santa Maria da Victoria. The city of Alcobaça will acquaint you with the love story of Pedro and Inês, and in Obidos we will have a city-holiday! And now some details. TOMAR. A city where every stone testifies to the long years of Portuguese history. The layout of the city has the shape of a cross, the ends of which are turned to the four parts of the world, and in the crosshairs lies the Republic Square. Accordingly, the main monuments of the city, the capital of the Templars, in particular the monasteries are located: the Monastery of the Order of Christ faces the West; the Monastery of Santa Iria faces the East; to the North is the Monastery of Anunciada and to the south is the Monastery of São Francisco. BATALLA. Leaving the motorway, you can notice how unexpectedly far away on the horizon there is the Monastery of Santa Maria da Vitoria. King Joan was the first to order the erection of this structure in memory of the brilliant victory over the Spaniards at Aljubarrota. This victory allowed the Portuguese to gain the freedom they had been striving for for many years. The city of Batalha grew up around the monastery. Thanks to its monumentality and rich decoration, the Monastery of Batalha is without a doubt one of the most striking examples of Gothic architecture in Portugal. ALCOBASA. Rapid progress has not left its mark on this city, which takes its name from the seemingly magical confluence of the two rivers Alcoa and Basa, along the banks of which its houses are spread. The city is dominated by the Monastery of Real Abadia de Santa Maria de Alcobaça, whose construction began in 1178. This Monastery will tell us about the eternal love of Pedro and Ines. OBIDUSH. The ancient city of Obidos served as an important seaport in ancient times, but over time, the river that connected the port with the sea changed its course, and this forever deprived the population of the city of its former functions. At the same time, its other characteristic has been preserved: Obidos has long been called the "city of weddings". Today, Obidos is a picturesque complex of whitewashed houses, decorated with bright flowers and standing on narrow medieval streets, surrounded by mighty fortress walls. However, the traveler approaching the city through the lush greenery of the countryside, with white spots of windmills, first of all draws attention to the sight that cannot be missed - the mighty castle, as if standing guard over the cluster of human buildings. The final chord of our journey will be the taste of cherry liqueur, which is drunk from chocolate cups. Prices are for an individual tour.
