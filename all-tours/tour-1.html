Embarking on a walking excursion of the capital city, with a particular focus on exploring its historically significant neighborhoods.

LISBON is one of the most beautiful cities in Europe with a transoceanic port of paramount importance. It used to be a Phoenician colony and the Romanesque capital of Lusitania. In 1225, Lisbon became the capital of the Kingdom of Portugal. It's better than not getting to know the city on foot. And the journey will start from Rossio Square. This is the center of the city, officially called Piazza Don Pedro IV. Here is a favorite meeting place for many citizens. In the center of the square is a statue of Pedro IV, Emperor of Brazil, erected in 1870. In the northern part of the square is the National Theater of Dona Maria II, with a classical peristyle, the work of Fortunato Lodi. After walking along the pedestrian street Augusta, we will make a stop at the Commerce Square. This is the most famous and largest square of the Portuguese capital, popularly called Terreiro Du Paso. Along the perimeter, this vast area is surrounded by identical buildings with porticos, built according to the project of Eugenio dos Santos. The center of the square is dominated by an equestrian monument to King Don Jos√© I, an elegant creation of Machado de Castro. To be in Lisbon and not look into Chiada? This name is commonly used when we talk about Garrett and Carmo streets, one of the most elegant streets in Lisbon and full of palaces, baroque churches. Nearby is the observation deck of St. Pedro de Alcantra, where we will definitely climb. We will get there on the Da Gloria tram, one of the traditional funiculars that travel along the steep slopes of the city. And then a meeting with the Castle of St. George: the residence of the kings at the dawn of the formation of the monarchy of Portugal. The Alfama district is located below the castle. This is a very picturesque Lisbon quarter, where houses form an array of intricate streets and flights of stairs, giving the area a folk flavor. Here in Alfama we will see the CE - Lisbon Cathedral and the center of the cardinal patriarchy of the city. The tour will end with a ride on the tram number 28 / historical route of the city.
