Lights of Lisbon

If you find yourself in the center of Largo do Chiado and look around, you can hardly see something very remarkable, you can rather feel it, especially in the evening, when all of Lisbon puts on a fancy dress. In this bohemian setting, it's hard to decide where to spend your time: in the theater of San Carlos, or in the open-air cafe Brasileira, or in the Bairro Alto tavern, or spend money on souvenirs in small shops. Maybe it's better to stay here to feel this romantic atmosphere? Let Camões decide for you, who has been standing in the center of the square for a long time, as an indispensable participant in this endless spectacle. Observation platforms, lanterns, lighting on the streets - all this will help create the mood for a walk around the city at night. Let's hit the road? After sunset, the Alfama area is given over to Fado. A stone's throw from the Patriarchal Cathedral, an exotic music lover can join the most famous genre of Portuguese song. Fado for Alfama is like a samovar for Tula. The music of ordinary people could only be born in such a typical quarter. There is no greater reward for a Portuguese poet than to see his work accepted by the world of fado players. Fernando Pessoa (1888-1935), one of the greatest Portuguese poets, wrote: An old song in a nearby tavern: How many similar listened to forever. I listen, staring into the dusk of the evening, And for no reason I fall into melancholy. Let me not know this old song, It doesn't matter, it doesn't matter at all. To the blood, the heart is wounded by a guitar, Tears ran out - otherwise I would cry. Called by whom and came from where This sadness, not mine and no one's? Everyone on earth is equally bad, The past is the eternal pain of being. Life ends, soon - in the dark. Sad song, sad news. There is only what is still there. (translated by E. Vitkovsky) There are fado performers whose fame has reached the most remote corners of the world. Among them, in the first place is Amalia Rodrigues, the greatest voice of fado, who sounded in the USSR at a time when there were not even diplomatic relations between our countries. Tours of the Portuguese pop singer Amalia Rodriguez were successfully held in Moscow, Leningrad, Tbilisi, Yerevan, Baku. Another fado singer, Mariza, not only inherited the qualities of Amalia, but also raised the skill of the "fadist" to a new level. Since November 27, 2011, fado has been on the UNESCO World Heritage List. After exploring Lisbon in the evening, you can go for dinner with fado.
