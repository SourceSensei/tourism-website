Sintra and Cape Roca / visiting from one to three palaces

Sintra is the most interesting thing to see within Greater Lisbon. Exotic parks, forests, castles, palaces, monasteries. The city in different centuries was a royal residence. A special microclimate, the beauty of nature is to blame. Sintra was included in the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1995 and attracts thousands of tourists throughout the year. Despite this, there are many quiet places to walk through the wooded hills. Hiking in Sintra is a lot of ascents and descents through steep hills. The historic center is the starting point of our journey, after which we will climb to the Pena Palace / a miracle of Portuguese architecture, then the Regaleira estate awaits us, where I will talk about the mysteries of Freemasonry. The Neo-Manueline manor house Regaleira with extensive gardens is a real find for a person interested in history and the occult. Pena Palace is an eclectic mix of architectural styles built in the 19th century. Our adventures will be crowned with a journey to the westernmost point of Europe - Cape Roca!
