Berlenga Island - the untouched nature of Portugal

Berlenga, despite the title of the island, is more like a rock that proudly rises among the ocean expanses. Waves and winds do not forgive the proud woman and attack the island with malice. We start from Lisbon. We arrive in the town of Peniche, which is located about 100 km from Lisbon. With the help of a shipping company, we get to the island by sea. / Please note that there are no ATMs on the island / . During your stay in the town of Peniche, you can try the wonderful local sweets Pasteis de Peniche, VERY DELICIOUS! Boarding the ship, a forty-minute journey, and we are at the goal! Beach, small hotel, a couple of cafes, a tent camp, etc. Spending a weekend on the island is a great idea! The island itself is the realm of birds. The water here has a greenish tint, the variety of fish is simply amazing, they can be observed even with the naked eye. A small military fortress has been perfectly preserved on the island - Fort Sao Joao Baptista built in 1502. In 2011, UNESCO included the island in the global list of natural reserves WNBR. THREE REASONS TO VISIT THE ISLAND: 1. The opportunity to plunge into the atmosphere of the untouched nature of Portugal. 2. This is one of the best places for bright windsurfing, surfing, diving and fishing. / You can rent equipment /. 3. The sea here is exceptionally transparent. The place is ideal for those who want to watch schools of sea bream, groupers and other fish, as well as explore caves and shipwrecks. The final chord of the trip can be lunch at a traditional local restaurant, where you just need to get acquainted with such a dish as caldeirada / abalone / ! Visit Berlenge!
