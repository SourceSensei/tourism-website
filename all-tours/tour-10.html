COIMBRA and the history of the first capital of Portugal

Coimbra was once the first capital of the kingdom of Portugal. It is currently the third city after Lisbon and Porto, from which Coimbra is at the same distance, on the right bank of the Mondego River. For about three centuries, the city shared with Lisbon the right to host the Portuguese University, one of the oldest in Europe. Finally, in 1537, at the request of King João III, the university was transferred and finally located in Coimbra, a city founded in 1290, but already existing in Roman times. The latter gave it the name Eminimum. Coimbra changed this ancient name after the neighboring village of Conimbriga, the largest settlement of Lusitania, was destroyed. In 468, the Suebi razed it to the surface of the earth, and the inhabitants of Eminium took its name and remade it in their own way. You should definitely visit the Ruins of Conimbriga, located 15 kilometers from Coimbra. Archaeological excavations at this site have unearthed magnificent mosaics, baths, houses, fragments of an aqueduct and the remains of a forum. The small local museum exhibits statues, coins, ceramics and other interesting finds. It can be argued that this is the largest archaeological deposit in Portugal. Coimbra is clearly divided into two social zones. The upper part of the city, where the clergy and nobility used to live, retains its medieval appearance. The lower part of the city, the place of concentration of artisans, merchants and ordinary citizens, looks completely different. In the upper part of the city are the Old Se Cathedral and the New Cathedral. The easiest and fastest way to get acquainted with the main attractions of the country is to visit the Portugal Park in miniature. Optionally, a visit to Coimbra can be combined with other attractions in Portugal.
