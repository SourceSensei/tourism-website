Nature and gastronomy of Portugal

First, we will cross the Tagus River on the April 25 Bridge. It has always been a problem for the inhabitants of Lisbon to move from one side of the river to the other. For many centuries, the crossing was carried out with the help of boats, and more recently - by ferry. All these problems were solved with the opening in 1966 of the "APRIL 25 BRIDGE", which is an outstanding achievement of engineering art. Driving across the bridge, it is impossible not to notice the statue of CHRIST - KING with outstretched arms, as if embracing and blessing the city. At your request, it is possible to stop at the famous statue. Well, then we will visit the elite village of Azeitau, where everyone who is at least a little interested in wines, cheeses and sweets of Portugal must visit! We will visit the largest wine producer in Portugal, Jose Maria de Fonseca, enjoy local cheeses and sweets! Fonseca winery produces quality table wines and is famous for its fragrant dessert wine Muscatel de Setúbal. They will tell you about the production of wine and show you old cellars with huge oak and chestnut barrels. At the end there is a wine tasting. JOSE MARIA DA FONSECA wines are sold in more than 75 countries around the world. The main plant is a closed area of ​​9000 m² with a production capacity of 6.5 million liters distributed over 513 tanks. Next, we will get acquainted with the protected area of ​​Arrábida. Arrábida Natural Park covers a small area of ​​limestone mountains lying east to south along the coast between Sesimbra and Setúbal. It was created to preserve the scenery and many species of birds and animals, including eagles, wild cats and badgers. Magnificent mountain and sea landscapes, viewing platforms and complete reunion with nature! Having walked a lot, hungry, a little tired and happy, we go for lunch to one of the simple traditional restaurants in the city of Setúbal. Setúbal is an important industrial city and the third largest port in Portugal. Fish Rodizio awaits us. This means that you will be offered more than 10 varieties of fish! Can you handle it? And at the end of our excursion day, acquaintance with the fortress of St. Philip in the city of Setubal. The fort in the form of a star was built in 1595 by Philip II during the reign of the Spaniards in order to resist pirates and English conquerors and control the local population. We return to Lisbon via the Vasco de Gamma bridge. An elegant and picturesque ferry linking Sakavem and Montijo. One of the longest bridges in Europe.
